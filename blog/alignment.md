# The AI Alignment Problem: A Smokescreen

Before discussing whether AI is "aligned" with human values, we must situate the technology within its socioeconomic context. The alignment debate, as currently framed, obscures a more basic fact: AI systems are produced, owned, and deployed against labour rights by monopoly capital.

## The Material Base

Training and operating large-scale AI requires investment that only a handful of corporations can mobilize. The technology rests on extractive practices: mass appropriation of human-generated data without consent or compensation; energy-intensive server infrastructure contributing to ecological damage; underpaid workers in the Global South labeling data under precarious conditions (Perrigo, 2023).

These are structural features, not aberrations.

## Monopoly Logic

Large technology firms resist regulation, oppose redistribution, and expand into unregulated niches to avoid oversight. Once established, they set prices, define terms, and control access. The goal is not market participation but market ownership.

Many of these firms built their businesses on the attention economy. Revenue depends on engagement, time spent, dependency. The model is addiction. This predates AI and now shapes its deployment.

Companies marketing "ethical AI" pitch investors on the same premise as their competitors: this technology will replace human labor. The ethical framing is instrumental.

## Anti-Democratic Structure

Large technology firms are not merely undemocratic. They are hostile to democratic institutions. They lobby against regulation, fund campaigns to undermine labor protections, and structure operations to evade accountability. This is not a question of whether they will align with democratic governance. They work to weaken it.

## Alignment as Misdirection

The alignment discourse asks how to ensure AI behaves ethically. This presupposes that ethical behavior at the model level resolves the problem.

A model that is polite, helpful, and refuses to generate certain content remains environmentally costly, built on extracted data, dependent on exploited labor. Its deployment still serves to automate jobs, weaken bargaining power of labour, deepen precarity. The model's internal ethics do not alter these facts.

The alignment problem redirects attention from who controls AI and toward what ends to the narrower question of how AI responds to prompts.

## Forced Implementation

Microsoft embeds Copilot into Windows. Apple integrates AI features into iOS. Google prepends AI summaries to search results. Consumers did not request these features. Each implementation consumes energy and water for no demonstrated benefit.

This is misalignment at the deployment level. A model can be as friendly as its designers wish. If the decision to force it into every interface disregards user autonomy and ecological limits, the system remains misaligned.

## Deskilling

AI, as currently deployed, does not cultivate autonomous activity. It renders users passive—consumers of generated content rather than producers of thought. When tools perform cognitive tasks, the capacity to perform those tasks atrophies. When interfaces optimize for engagement, users become less capable of independent judgment.

A technology aligned with human welfare would make itself progressively unnecessary. No major AI company pursues this.

## Structural Alignment

If alignment means anything, it must operate at multiple levels:

1. **Individual**: The model's outputs relative to stated values.
2. **Implementation**: Deployment decisions respecting autonomy and ecological limits.
3. **Structural**: The technology's relationship to human welfare broadly—democratic institutions, labor rights, peace, ecological stability, dignified conditions of life.

The first level without the others is meaningless. An image model that refuses certain outputs but floods the internet with generated content—consuming resources, degrading information ecosystems—is not aligned. A helpful assistant deployed by firms lobbying against labor protections is not aligned.

Current alignment discourse addresses only the first level. The structural level is not merely neglected. It cannot be addressed within the present system of production.

A model's politeness, its refusals to put people in a bikini, its helpfulness: none of this matters if the technology erodes democratic capacity, degrades ecological systems, and concentrates power.

There is no ethical consumption under capitalism.
